# Odd Tori `d < 29` Boundary Witness Table

Date: 2026-05-03.

This note records the finite boundary audit for the global odd-tori
formalization goal.  The boundary under discussion is:

```text
d odd, 3 <= d < 29
m odd, 3 <= m < d
```

There are exactly `78` such pairs.  With uniform seed dimensions

```text
2, 3, 5, 7
```

and the base-tail Hall-slack theorem, no pair remains unresolved.

## Seed Semigroup

The multiplicative semigroup generated by `{2,3,5,7}` contains the following
dimensions up to `28`:

```text

```

```text

```

The remaining odd dimensions below `29` are:

```text
11, 13, 17, 19, 23
```

These are handled by base-tail witnesses.

## Unit Partitions

For every odd `m >= 3`, the only summand sizes used in the finite boundary are
`2` and `3`:

```text
2 = 1 + (m - 1)
3 = 1 + 1 + (m - 2)
```

All parts are positive units modulo odd `m`.

## Base-Tail Witness Details

For the base-tail rows, the Hall-slack inequality is hardest at `m = 3`.
Since `m^(b) > m d T` is equivalent to `m^(b-1) > dT`, the left side increases
with odd `m >= 3`.

| `d` | `b` | `T=d-b` | composition of `d` into `b` summands | worst slack check |
|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| 11 | 5 | 6 | `3+2+2+2+2` | `3^5 = 243 > 3*11*6 = 198` |
| 13 | 6 | 7 | `3+2+2+2+2+2` | `3^6 = 729 > 3*13*7 = 273` |
| 17 | 6 | 11 | `3+3+3+3+3+2` | `3^6 = 729 > 3*17*11 = 561` |
| 19 | 7 | 12 | `3+3+3+3+3+2+2` | `3^7 = 2187 > 3*19*12 = 684` |
| 23 | 8 | 15 | `3+3+3+3+3+3+3+2` | `3^8 = 6561 > 3*23*15 = 1035` |

In each row:

```text
b < d
T = d - b > b
base dimension b is in the seed semigroup
d is a sum of b integers, each equal to 2 or 3
each 2 or 3 is realized by positive units modulo odd m
m^b > m d T for every odd m in the listed range
```
